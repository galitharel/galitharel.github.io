Why Affirmations Don't Always Feel True

If you've repeated the words but something inside you says "no," here's what may actually be happening.

You repeat: "I am confident." But something inside you says: "No, you're not."

You say: "I am abundant." And another part of you immediately responds: "That's not true."

You may think this means you need to repeat the affirmation more.

But sometimes, the problem isn't the affirmation.

It's the gap between the words you are saying and the experience your body knows.

Your mind can understand a new idea. But if the feeling is completely unfamiliar, your body may resist it.

Not because you are broken. Not because you are incapable of change.

Because something new can feel difficult to trust.

This is why forcing yourself to believe something can create even more inner conflict.

Instead of jumping from "I don't feel this at all" to "This is completely true," you can begin creating a bridge.

A new feeling can become familiar gradually.

You don't have to force yourself to believe something your whole system is not ready to receive.

You can train the feeling.
