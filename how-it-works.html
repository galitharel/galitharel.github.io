How It Works

Change doesn't happen
in one giant leap.
It happens through a sequence.

The I Love Method™ helps you move from automatic reaction to conscious choice — and from conscious choice to a new way of being.

1 · Automatic Reaction

First, you notice
what is already happening.

A situation happens. Your body reacts. A familiar thought appears. You respond.

Often before you even realize you had a choice.

Trigger → Pattern → Reaction

This is where most change begins: Awareness.

2 · Awareness

You can't change
what you can't see.

You begin to notice:

✓
What triggers you
✓
What you feel
✓
What you think
✓
What you automatically do

Not to judge yourself.

To create a moment of choice.

3 · Safety

Your body needs a reason
to trust something new.

A new thought. A new behavior. A new identity.

If something feels too unfamiliar, your system may pull you back toward what it already knows.

The answer isn't always more pressure.

Sometimes, the next step is creating enough safety to stay with something new.

4 · Choice

This is where the pattern
begins to change.

You notice the old response. You create space. And instead of automatically repeating the same pattern…

You have another option.

Not perfection. Not a dramatic transformation overnight. A different choice.

5 · Train the Feeling

A new identity begins
with a new experience.

You don't just repeat: "I am confident."

You begin to become familiar with the experience of confidence.

You don't just say: "I am safe."

You begin to create a felt experience of safety.

What becomes familiar becomes easier to return to.

6 · Integration

Change becomes real
through everyday life.

The new response gets practiced. The new feeling becomes more familiar. The new choice becomes easier to access.

And over time…

What once felt unfamiliar begins to feel like you.

The Important Part

You don't have
to do this perfectly.

You will still have old reactions. You will still have difficult days. You may still return to familiar patterns.

The goal is not to never go back.

The goal is to know how to find your way forward again.

Final Thought

This is what you learn to practice.

One moment. One choice. One new feeling. At a time.
