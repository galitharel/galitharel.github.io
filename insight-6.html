Why Trying Harder Doesn't Always Create Change

Sometimes the answer isn't more effort.

You want to change. So you try harder.

You read more. Learn more. Plan more. Push more. Promise yourself that this time will be different.

And sometimes, for a while, it is.

Until you find yourself back in the same pattern.

Then you think: "I just need to try harder."

But what if more pressure is not what you need?

Sometimes, trying harder creates more internal conflict.

One part of you wants to move forward. Another part of you is still trying to protect what feels familiar.

The answer may not be forcing one part to defeat the other.

It may be learning how to work with yourself.

Lasting change doesn't always require more effort.

Sometimes it requires a different relationship with the effort you're already making.

Train the feeling. Everything else follows.
